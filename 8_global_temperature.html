Evaluating the evidence for a “Pause” in warming?

The 2013 IPCC Report included a tentative observation of a “much smaller increasing trend” in global mean temperatures since 1998 than was observed previously. This led to much discussion in the media about the existence of a “Pause” or “Hiatus” in global warming rates, as well as much research looking into where the extra heat could have gone. (Examples discussing this question include articles in The Guardian, BBC News, and Wikipedia).

plot(No_Smoothing ~ Year, data=temp_anomaly, ylab="Global mean temperature anomaly") +

Calculating rolling averages

In the last lesson we calculated annual averages and 5 year averages to get a smoother look at our data and be less susceptible to random perturbations. In that lesson, we defined the 5 year average as the average of the data from a given year along with the 2 years before and 2 years after (i.e. the 5-year rolling average of 2005 temperature = the temperature mean from 2003-2007).

Sometimes scientists calculate moving averages where they simply just look at the period prior to a given year. For example, we could define a 5 year average as the average over a given year and the 4 years prior (i.e. the 5-year rolling average of 2005 temperature = the temperature from 2001-2005). Either definition is acceptable as long as you are explicit with which definition you are using. The advantage of calculating moving averages for an endpoint (instead of a midpoint) is that they are simpler to calculate and you can provide averages up to the most current date. For this lesson, we’ll calculate moving averages using the simpler end-point definition.

Let’s create a user defined function that will calculate the moving average for any vector of numbers, and the user can choose what the size of the moving window will be (i.e. whether it will be a 1-year average, 5-year average, 10-year average, etc.).

# User defined function to calculate a rolling average for a user-specified moving window
# The rolling averages are calculated with the reference year as the ENDPOINT for the moving window
# Averages are not calculated for data points prior to the first time point when the full moving window can be included in the average
# Default size of the moving window is set at 5

calc_rolling_avg = function(data, moving_window=5){ 
  result = rep(NA, length(data))
  for (i in c(moving_window:length(data))){
    result[i] = mean(data[seq(from=(i-moving_window), to=i)])
  }
  return(result)
}

result_avg_5_yr = calc_rolling_avg(temp_anomaly$No_Smoothing)
temp_anomaly_roll_avg = cbind(temp_anomaly, avg_5_yr = result_avg_5_yr)

To use this function in future code, you can copy and paste the function at the top of your new R scripts.
